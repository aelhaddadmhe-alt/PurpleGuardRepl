With it, you gain a proactive, always-on early warning system that helps you <strong>reduce exposures, strengthen defenses, and stay ahead of cyber threats.</strong>&nbsp;</p></div>

</div>

<button type="button" class="accordion-header accordion-button justify-content-between gap-2 bg-transparent h6-fs fw-bold text-decoration-none text-reset transition-none collapsed" data-bs-toggle="collapse" aria-expanded="false" id="myCollapseBtn1752750205981" data-bs-target="#myCollapseTab1752750205980" aria-controls="myCollapseTab1752750205980"><span class="flex-grow-1">

What constitutes an organization's external attack surface?

</span></button>

<div class="accordion-collapse collapse" data-bs-parent="#myCollapse1752750205977" role="region" id="myCollapseTab1752750205980" aria-labelledby="myCollapseBtn1752750205981">

<div class="accordion-body">

<p>An organization’s <strong>external attack surface</strong> is <strong>everything connected to the internet that an attacker can see, probe, and potentially exploit.&nbsp;</strong>This can include websites, email servers, cloud storage and services, web applications, APIs, Internet of Things (IoT) devices, and all <strong>public-facing assets, services, and exposures — whether known or unknown — that could become entry points into your organization.</strong></p><p>Attackers only need <strong>one weak spot</strong> to break in.

&nbsp;</p><p>

</p>

</div>

</div>

</div><div class="accordion-item position-relative z-1" data-name="Accordion Item">

<button type="button" class="accordion-header accordion-button justify-content-between gap-2 bg-transparent h6-fs fw-bold text-decoration-none text-reset transition-none collapsed" data-bs-toggle="collapse" aria-expanded="false" id="myCollapseBtn1752871352174" data-bs-target="#myCollapseTab1752871352173" aria-controls="myCollapseTab1752871352173"><span class="flex-grow-1">

What is Digital Footprint?

&nbsp;<br/></span></button>

<div class="accordion-collapse collapse" data-bs-parent="#myCollapse1752750205977" role="region" id="myCollapseTab1752871352173" aria-labelledby="myCollapseBtn1752871352174">

<div class="accordion-body">

<p>A <strong>digital footprint</strong> is the complete record of all the online assets, activities, and traces an organization leaves across the internet — both intentionally and unintentionally.</p><p>To manage the attack surface, it is necessary to first identify all assets open to the Internet. The digital footprint is important because companies have many assets that they do not know or forget, as well as assets they know and manage. For instance, some promotional pages opened for marketing purposes may have been forgotten to shut down or not notified to the security team. Any assets that are forgotten or not configured for security threats could be harmful to companies. Because attackers always prefer to attack companies over unmanaged assets.

&nbsp;</p><p>

Your digital footprint defines <strong>how you appear to the outside world — including customers, partners, and attackers.&nbsp;</strong>Managing it is essential to reducing risk and improving your security posture.

</p><p>

</p>

</div>

<button type="button" class="accordion-header accordion-button justify-content-between gap-2 bg-transparent h6-fs fw-bold text-decoration-none text-reset transition-none collapsed" data-bs-toggle="collapse" aria-expanded="false" id="myCollapseBtn1752871505362" data-bs-target="#myCollapseTab1752871505361" aria-controls="myCollapseTab1752871505361"><span class="flex-grow-1">

How can an organization reduce its external attack surface?

&nbsp;<br/></span></button>

<div class="accordion-collapse collapse" data-bs-parent="#myCollapse1752750205977" role="region" id="myCollapseTab1752871505361" aria-labelledby="myCollapseBtn1752871505362">

<div class="accordion-body">

<p>

Organizations can reduce their external attack surface by regularly conducting vulnerability assessments and penetration testing, timely patching and updating software, employing strong access control measures, securing APIs, encrypting data, and utilizing other cybersecurity best practices.

</p><p>

</p>
